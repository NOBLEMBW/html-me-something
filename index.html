Crater Lake, Oregon

The deep blue crystal clear waters that fill the caldera of the sunken volcano Mount Mazama help make Crater Lake one of America's most beautiful lakes.Crater Lake, located in Southern Oregon, is also the deepest lake in the United States, plunging to depths of 1,943 feet, with sunlight extending 400 feet down.

The Maldives

The Maldives is one of the world's best islands.This low-lying island nation resides between the Indian and Arabian seas. It boasts of luxuriously appointed bungalows set over dazzling blue waters and is surrounded by white sand beaches.

Havelock Island, India

This far-flung island paradise is part of the chain of Andaman Islands in the Bay of Bengal.Havelock Island is a great tropical getaway that is completely off the grid. It's not easy to reach, but it's worth the trip for anyone who craves snow white beaches and aquamarine waters.

Peyto Lake, Alberta, Canada

This lake that dwells in Banff National Park owes it's brilliant blue hue to the icy meltwater and silt from the Peyto Glacier and Wapta Icefields. The sapphire-blue lake is best viewed from the Bow Summit near Alberta's Lake Louise. Most visitors take postcard shots of the gem colored lake here.The natural hot springs at Banff allows one to rest off sore muscles.

Zanzibar, Tanzania

Located in the Indian Ocean 15 miles off the coast of Tanzania,the Zanzibar Archipelago is a breathtaking spot to escape from the world. Prepare to enjoy clear, turquoise-blue water, shallow sandbars and many small, nearly deserted islands virtually unvisited by tourists.
